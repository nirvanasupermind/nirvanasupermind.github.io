Replace Decimal With Sexagesimal

Like how our decimal system is structured around the powers of 10, the sexagesimal (base 60) numeral system is structured around the powers of 60. Counting starts at zero and is normal through 59. After 59 is 01 00 (two sexagesimal digits), and the last digit again counts up to 59 until reaching 01 59. After 59 59 is 01 00 00, and so forth.

Here are some examples of sexagesimal numbers:

6510 = 01 0560 = 1 × 60 + 5

26210 = 04 2260 = 4 × 60 + 22

456610 = 01 06 0660 = 1 × 602 + 16 × 60 + 6

Here, decimal numbers are marked with a subscript 10 and sexagesimal ones with a subscript 60. The number 60 can be divided by 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 15, 20, and 30, whereas 10 can only be divided by 1, 2, 5, and 10. This makes multiplication and division much easier in sexagesimal, with repeating fractions such as "1/3 = 0.333..." in decimal being substantially less common.
