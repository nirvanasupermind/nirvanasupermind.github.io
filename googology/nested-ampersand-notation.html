Nested Ampersand Notation

Nested Ampersand Notation (N&N) is the third part of Ampersand Notation.

Definition

Define an ampersand-structure as an expression of the form &@x, where @ is any sequence of ampersand-structures, and x is a non-negative integer. x is optional and may be removed.

The number of layers in an ampersand-structure is the number of nested subscripts, e.g. & has 0 layers, &5 has 1 layer, and &&7 has 2 layers.

A valid expression in N&N is of the form a[@b], where @ is any sequence of ampersand structures, and a and b are non-negative integers. b is optional and may be removed. N&N outputs a non-negative integer for every valid expression, determined using this set of rules:

  1. a[] = aa
  2. a[@0]= a[@]
  3. b > 0: a[@b]= a[@b - 1][@b - 1]...[@b - 1][@b - 1], with a copies of "[@b - 1]"
  4. # is another ampersand-structure sequence: a[@&&...#0] with x layers in the ampersand-structure after @ = a[@&&...#] with x layers in the ampersand-structure after @
  5. # is another ampersand-structure sequence: a[@&&...#&] with x layers in the ampersand-structure after @ = a[@&&...#a] with x layers in the ampersand-structure after @
  6. # is another ampersand structure sequence, y > 0: a[@&&...#x] with y layers in the ampersand-structure after @ = a[@&&...&#x - 1&#x - 1...&#x - 1&#x - 1] with y + 1 layers in the ampersand-structure after @ and a copies of "#x - 1"

Examples

2[&&1]
= 2[&&][&&]
= 2[&2][&&]
= 2[&1]\&1][&&]
= 2[&0][&0][&1][&&]
= 2[&][&][&1][&&]
= 2[2][&][&1][&&]
= 2[1][1][&][&1][&&]
= ...
